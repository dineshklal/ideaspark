Founder protections

Ideas are timestamped on publication. Backers pledge intent — money only moves through regulated funding partners with full disclosures. Investors sign platform terms prohibiting misappropriation. Nothing here replaces your own NDA/patent strategy: publish the what, keep the how.

Trust & Safety

Content moderation. Every submission passes automated screening (scam patterns, prohibited categories, PII leaks) plus human review before going public. Anyone can flag content; 3 flags auto-hides an idea pending re-review.
Prohibited: securities offers outside regulated channels, guaranteed-return claims, medical/health claims without evidence, weapons, illegal goods or services, MLM/pyramid structures, impersonation, and ideas that infringe known IP.
Payments. Card data never touches IdeaSpark servers — processing is delegated to Stripe (PCI-DSS Level 1). Reward pre-orders are purchases, not investments; refundable until the founder ships. Investment money moves only through registered funding portals (Reg CF) or between parties directly (Reg D) — never through IdeaSpark.
Investor verification. Investors attest accreditation status, verify identity (KYC), and are approved manually before appearing in the directory or receiving founder contact details.
Data. Minimal collection, no sale of personal data, deletion on request (CCPA/GDPR). All actions on ideas, pledges, and intros are logged to an immutable audit trail.
IdeaSpark — demo prototype. Pledges and investor intros are simulated; no real money moves.
Production launch of any fundraising feature requires securities-law compliance (e.g., SEC Regulation Crowdfunding via a registered funding portal, or Reg D for accredited investors). Consult a securities attorney before accepting funds.
